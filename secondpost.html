Everyday violence, structural racism and mistreatment at the US–Mexico border

This piece dives into the unfortunate reality of the mistreatment, everyday violence, and structural racism faced by thousands of individuals at the US-Mexico border.

Posted by Aidan Dungan on April 28, 2023

"Everyday Violence, Structural Racism and Mistreatment at the US–Mexico Border" by Sabo, Samantha, et al.

The prevalence of mistreatment related to immigration enforcement policy and militarization along the US-Mexico border, should be a revelation to no one. Approximately 25% of US citizens and permanent residents of Mexican heritage reported mistreatment connected to immigration, with 62% of those individuals experiencing personal victimization, according to a research done in the Arizona border region between 2006 and 2008 (Sabo et al., 2014). The majority of maltreatment took place outside of US ports of entry, emphasizing how widespread immigration enforcement practices and militarization in daily life are.

The population's acceptance of mistreatment resulting from immigration shows that institutionalized discrimination practices like ethno-racial profiling and mistreatment are examples of structural racism and commonplace violence. According to academics, monitoring methods should be set up to track how such enforcement actions affect the daily lives of US citizens of Mexican descent who are permanent residents as well as their co-ethnic non-immigrant US citizens.
